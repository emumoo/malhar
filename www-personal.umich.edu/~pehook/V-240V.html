Notes on the compound verb in<font size="+1" face="Xdvng">O</font> and the expression of insistence.

      Adding the invariant ending -O to the first part of a transitive compound verb lends a note of insistence or even stubbornness to an expression:

 1.  g:l:a s:ÜK:a j:a rha hò Aaòr t:Ü s:_a p:an:i ep:l:aO dðt:i hò !
      'My throat is drying up and you insist on giving me rotten water to drink!'

      (from Premchand's YakÙr ka kÙAaú: context.)

 2.  n:ay:k t:iv:Òt:a kñ s:aT: b:aðl:a, ' hm: AB:i s:b: dðK:ð l:ðt:ð hòö. '
      'The officer said sharply, "We'll see about all of that!"'

      (from Vrindavan Lal Verma's  XÞXð kaúXð, p. 18)

The suffix -O may be used by speakers (especially with the verb  kh dð 'say') as a way of indicating that they will not take 'no' for an answer. Example (3) is from a story where a low caste woman is intent on staking a prior claim to a reward for becoming wetnurse to the baby boy of a landowner:

 3.  b:hÝj:i,  m:Üû_n: m:ðö c:Ü_ð l:Üúg:i khð dðt:i hÝú !
      'Bahuji, you'll give me heavy bangles at his Munj (tonsure ceremony). I will have them, I tell you.'

      (from Premchand's dÜD: ka dam:: context.)

 Compare example [3] with [4] in which the insistent tone is absent - as is the -O on kh- in kh dðt:a hÞú :

 4.   m:òø kh dðt:a hÜú, kB:i em:l:ðög:ð t:að khÜúg:a.
      'I tell [her], if I meet [him], I'll tell [him] [to drop by].'

      (from Premchand's g:aðdan:: context.)

Adding -O  to the first part of a compound verb may also often give a sense of imminence or immediate future:

 5.  Aaj: hi K:ðt: ki kXaI AarmB: krð dðt:a hÝú, AeD:k Yhrn:ð s:ð Aaðl:ð-p:an:i ka s:ökX es:r p:r Aa s:kt:a hò ...
      'I will start the harvest right from today. If I wait any longer I may have rain or hail to contend with.'

      (from Vrindavan Lal Verma's XÝXð kaúXð, p. 11)

At times the -O simply indicates "nearly" (6) or supports subordinating conjunctions having the meaning of "as if", such as j:òs:ð or m:an:aðö (7). The effect is to hedge or pull back slightly from what would otherwise be an implausible assertion or exaggeration:

 6.  Et:n:ð j:an:v:raðö kað Vy:a eK:l:av:ðö, y:hi ec:nt:a m:arð Ral:t:i hò.
      'I'm worried to death. What shall I feed so many animals? '

      (from Premchand's g:aðdan:: context.)

 7.  us:kñ Ant:HkrN: s:ð j:òs:ð AaS:iv:aüdaðö ka vy:Üh-s:a en:kl: kr haðri kað Ap:n:ð Andr eCp:ay:ð l:ðt:a T:a
      'As if a storm of blessings emerged from her heart to conceal [and protect] Hori.'

      (from Premchand's g:aðdan:: context.)

It is to be noted that the addition of -O to the first (non-finite) verb in a serial sequence (ie, two verbs in a sequence that together express two closely sequenced actions) has the same effect as adding -O to the main verb in a compound verb. In (8) and (9) speakers refer to complex actions that they will carry out right away:

 8.  y:h p:an:i kòs:ð ep:y:aðg:ð ?  n: j:an:ð kaòn: j:an:v:r m:ra hò.  kÙOú s:ð m:òö dÜs:ra p:an:i l:aO dðt:i hÝú.
      'How will you drink this water. Who knows what animal died in it! I'll go get more water from the well.'

     (from Premchand's YakÙr ka kÙAaú: context.)

&#nbsp;9.  Ab: y:haú A:kr m:òön:ð dðK: el:y:a ek A:p:kð G:r m:ðö m:ðrð el:y:ð j:g:h hað s:kt:i hò. Es:el:y:ð m:òö s:am:an: uYaO l:at:a hÞú .
      'Now that I'm here I see your house has room for me. So I'll just get [= pick up and bring] my things.'

      (online: from bottom of page 6 of Shaokat Thanvi's B:aB:i: context.)

The compound [or serial] verb is itself almost always in the present habitual form.

A more detailed discussion of the compound verb in -O and the expression of insistence or imminence can be found in Hook 1974:261-7.
